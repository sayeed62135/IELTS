How to Score Band 8+ in IELTS Writing Task 2

Sarah Johnson November 10, 2025 5 min read 24 Comments

Scoring Band 8 or above in IELTS Writing Task 2 requires more than just good English — it demands structure, coherence, and advanced vocabulary used naturally.

1. Understand the Question Fully

Many students lose marks because they misinterpret the question. Always identify:

  • Question type (Opinion, Discussion, Problem-Solution, etc.)
  • Key instruction words (Discuss, Evaluate, To what extent)
  • All parts of the question

2. Plan Your Essay in 5 Minutes

High-band essays are well-organized. Spend 5 minutes planning:

  • Introduction: Paraphrase + thesis
  • Body 1: Main point + example
  • Body 2: Counterpoint or second idea
  • Conclusion: Summary + final thought

3. Use Advanced Vocabulary Naturally

Avoid overusing complex words. Use collocations and topic-specific terms:

  • Instead of "very important" → crucial, paramount, pivotal
  • Instead of "many people" → a significant proportion of the population

4. Vary Sentence Structures

Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences. Use linking words smoothly:

  • Addition: furthermore, in addition
  • Contrast: however, nevertheless
  • Cause/Effect: consequently, as a result

5. Write a Strong Conclusion

Restate your position and add a final insight. Avoid new ideas.

Pro Tip: Practice writing one Task 2 essay daily and get feedback from a teacher or use our AI essay checker.
