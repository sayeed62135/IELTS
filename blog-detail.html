How to Score Band 8+ in IELTS Writing Task 2

Sarah Johnson
IELTS Examiner & Trainer
November 10, 2025 7 min read 24 Comments

Scoring Band 8 or above in IELTS Writing Task 2 is not about perfect English — it's about strategic writing that meets examiner expectations in all four criteria: Task Response, Coherence, Lexical Resource, and Grammar.

1. Understand the Question Like an Examiner

Most Band 6–7 candidates lose marks here. Always ask:

  • What type is the question? (Opinion, Discussion, Advantages/Disadvantages, Problem-Solution)
  • What are the keywords? Underline them.
  • Are there two parts? Never ignore one.

“A Band 8 response fully addresses all parts of the task with a well-developed position.” — IELTS Public Band Descriptors

2. Plan in 4 Minutes — Never Skip This

High-band essays are organized from the first word. Use this structure:

Introduction: Paraphrase question + clear thesis

Body 1: Main idea + explanation + example

Body 2: Second idea or counterargument

Conclusion: Summarize + final insight

3. Use Band 8 Vocabulary Naturally

Forget word lists. Use collocations and topic-specific terms:

  • paramount importance → not "very important"
  • exacerbate the issue → not "make problem worse"
  • a multifaceted approach → shows range

4. Master Cohesion & Paragraphing

Link ideas smoothly:

  • Addition: moreover, furthermore
  • Contrast: however, nevertheless, on the contrary
  • Cause/Effect: consequently, as a result

5. Grammar: Variety + Accuracy

Mix sentence types:


Although technology has revolutionized education, 
many argue that traditional teaching methods remain essential.

Pro Tip: Write one Task 2 essay daily. Get feedback from a trainer or use our AI Essay Checker for instant band score.

Final Checklist Before Submission

  • Word count: 250+?
  • Clear position throughout?
  • Examples relevant?
  • No repetition of ideas?
  • Spelling & grammar checked?
